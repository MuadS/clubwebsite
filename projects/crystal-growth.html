Crystal Growth Prediction

Machine learning model that predicts crystal formation patterns and growth rates for materials science applications

Materials Science Simulation Regression Random Forest
R² = 0.87 10K+ Samples

Problem & Dataset

The Challenge

Crystal growth prediction is crucial for materials science applications, from semiconductor manufacturing to pharmaceutical drug development. Traditional methods rely on expensive and time-consuming experimental trials or computationally intensive molecular dynamics simulations.

Our machine learning approach provides rapid predictions of crystal formation patterns and growth rates based on molecular properties and environmental conditions, enabling researchers to optimize synthesis conditions before conducting physical experiments.

Dataset Overview

We compiled a comprehensive dataset from multiple sources including the Materials Project database, experimental crystallization studies, and molecular dynamics simulations. The dataset includes molecular descriptors, environmental parameters, and corresponding crystal growth measurements.

Method

Feature Engineering Pipeline

Molecular descriptor calculation and feature selection process

Approach

We developed an ensemble machine learning approach combining Random Forest regression with gradient boosting methods. The model takes molecular descriptors and environmental parameters as input to predict crystal growth rates and formation energies.

Model Architecture

Our ensemble model consists of three main components: a Random Forest regressor for capturing non-linear relationships, a Gradient Boosting regressor for handling complex interactions, and a linear regression model for baseline predictions. The final prediction is a weighted average of all three models.

Training Process

Feature engineering involved calculating molecular descriptors using RDKit, including topological indices, electronic properties, and thermodynamic parameters. We employed cross-validation with stratified sampling to ensure robust performance across different crystal systems.

Key Features:

  • Comprehensive molecular descriptor calculation (SMILES to features)
  • Multi-target prediction for growth rate and formation energy
  • Uncertainty quantification through ensemble variance
  • Interpretable feature importance analysis

Code Example

from crystal_predictor import CrystalGrowthModel

# Initialize the model
model = CrystalGrowthModel()

# Predict crystal growth for aspirin
smiles = "CC(=O)OC1=CC=CC=C1C(=O)O"
conditions = {
    'temperature': 298.15,  # K
    'pressure': 1.0,        # atm
    'solvent': 'water',
    'concentration': 0.1    # M
}

prediction = model.predict(smiles, conditions)
print(f"Growth rate: {prediction['growth_rate']:.3f} mm/day")
print(f"Formation energy: {prediction['formation_energy']:.2f} kJ/mol")
